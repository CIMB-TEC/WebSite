ADMAS

See Projects

The proyect Advanced Driver Monitoring for Assistance System (ADMAS) tries to develop a new driving asistance service that works in an efficient way to a driver, depending on his/hers psycho-physical state.

The system pretends to convert a Level 2 autonomous car, into a level 3 or 4, in other words, transforming a common car into a semi-autonomous one. This will be achived by implemmenting sensors and artificial intelligence algorithms that take control of the vehicle when the driver is not able to drive safely.

To determine the psycho-physical state od the driver, the vehicle will have pre-isntalled biometric sensors that will measure phisical variables to determine the health state, and the emotions that the driver presents.

The fusion of these sensors, will allow to determine if the vehicle should take the roll of the driver, in case that the driver's actions are not propper to a safe driving, or when any mental or health state, seems to be affected.

Emotional Domotics

See Projects

This projects seeks to develop and establish a liveable space, in which the emotions can be suggested by means of ambiental variables and pre-programmed positive estimulation.

It uses a facial expresion's detection system, an electroencephalography system, that detects the brain's electrical responeses and a device that measures the electric restistance of the user's skin, all of these with the purpose of stablishing the phyhsiolocical and emocional state of the subject.

With the subject state, variations in the enviroment occur(temperature, humidity, light intensity and tone) to take the subject to a desire state.

A good example is to try to transition between being angry or sad to being neutral or happy, these results could be applied in an office, to improve the employees life quality and therefore their health, and by improving these, the system could also improve their efficiency

The research areas that have been involved in the project are: Mechatronics, Telecomunications and Electrical Systems, Computer Science, Industrial Engeeniering, Biotechnology, Industrial Design, Architecture, Psychology, Marketing, and Estadistics

Acustic Agriculture

The purpose of this project is to develop a space completely autonomous and isolated to study the vegetal growth, where environmental conditions could be measured constantly, and changed if needed to allow the growth of different plants. This system is a sustainable alternative to traditional agriculture, using acoustic stimulation to speed up the growth and production of vegetal specimens, eradicating the need of pesticide and fertilizer, also reducing the amount of water used to irrigate the plant.

The system analyzes the environment in which the specimen will be exposed, by means of one temperature and humidity sensor and two sensors to check the earths humidity. For its development, it regulates the light, with a led light; and a fan to exchange gases from the inside to the outside of the space. Besides it has an audio system to expose the specimen to different types of acoustic stimulation, from specific frequencies to a recording.

From this results, a comparison between a control specimen, one that has not been affected by any means; and the acoustic stimulated, to see the effect, either positive or negative, on growth rate and production. This is done to determine which conditions are better for the specimen, therefor more production, leading to more earnings in the same amount of time.

This is a multi-discplinary project, with the integration of different areas to get a better result. Until now students of the careers of Biothecnology, Telecomunications and Electronic Systems, Mechatronics, Computers Science, Industrial Design, have participated.
